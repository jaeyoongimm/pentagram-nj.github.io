About the Project

Located at the former headquarters of the Ontario Association of Architects, RavinePortal presents architectural, landscape and artistic interventions that address the physical and psychological disconnect between Toronto's dense urban environment and the 26,000 acres of ravine landscape that shape the city. The site, now a private architecture office situated within the Rosedale Valley Ravine less than 500 yards from the intersection of Yonge Street and Bloor Street, is Toronto's most prominent commercial address and main subway interchange. The exhibition opens the space to the public and explores the past and present role the ravines play in the life of the city, and the future potential of the ravine system as a defining, interconnected and accessible open space and cultural network. The exhibition program includes site-specific installations, tours, lectures, and collaborations between artists and the general public that demonstrates how to connect citizens of Toronto to the ravines in a way that is meaningful and sustainable.
